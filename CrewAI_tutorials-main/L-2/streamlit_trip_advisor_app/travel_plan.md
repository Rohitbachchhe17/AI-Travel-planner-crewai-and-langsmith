# Itinerary for Rome (May 2026)

## Overview
Welcome to Rome! May is a fantastic month to explore the Eternal City, with pleasant weather averaging between 15°C to 25°C. The city is rich in history, architecture, and offers a culinary landscape that is both diverse and delectable. This itinerary caters to those seeking to immerse themselves in Rome's monumental sights and its exquisite food scene.

Rome, often referred to as the "Eternal City," is a place where history is present at every corner. Walk through the winding streets and discover ancient ruins, magnificent churches, and piazzas filled with fountains. Each neighborhood has its distinct character, from the bustling Trastevere with its vibrant nightlife to the historical Center packed with iconic landmarks like the Colosseum and the Vatican.

Food lovers will find themselves indulged in Roman culinary delights such as traditional pasta dishes, gelato, and wood-fired pizza. May also presents an opportunity to experience local festivals and food events, giving visitors a taste of Roman culture beyond the usual tourist attractions.

This carefully structured itinerary will guide you through a month-long adventure filled with sightseeing, gastronomic experiences, and local events. Prepare for an unforgettable trip as you embrace the charm and flavor of Rome.

## Accommodation
- **Types of Accommodation**: 
  - Mid-range hotels (Average €150 per night)
  - Hostels available for budget travelers
  - Rentals via Airbnb offer a local experience
- **Recommendations**: Book early to secure a place in the historic center.

## Daily Travel Plan

### Day 1: Arrival & Initial Exploration
- **Morning (10 AM)**: Arrive at Fiumicino Airport. Take a shuttle or taxi to your hotel.
- **Afternoon (1 PM)**: Explore **Piazza Navona** and enjoy gelato from **Gelateria del Teatro**.
- **Evening (6 PM)**: Dinner at **Trattoria Da Enzo al 29** for authentic Roman dishes.

### Day 2: Ancient Rome
- **Morning (9 AM)**: Visit the **Colosseum** and **Roman Forum** (€25).
- **Lunch (1 PM)**: Roman-style pizza at **Pizzeria Ai Marmi**.
- **Afternoon (3 PM)**: Tour of **Palatine Hill**.
- **Evening (7 PM)**: Dinner at **Osteria Bonelli** for classic pasta.

### Day 3: Vatican City
- **Morning (9 AM)**: Explore **St. Peter's Basilica** (free, dome climb €10).
- **Lunch (1 PM)**: Local favorites at **Pizzarium** (famous for pizza al taglio).
- **Afternoon (3 PM)**: Vatican Museums tour (€17).
- **Evening (8 PM)**: Traditional meal at **La Città dell'Altro Mondo**.

### Day 4: Gardens & Culture
- **Morning (10 AM)**: Stroll through **Borghese Gardens** (free entry).
- **Lunch (1 PM)**: Desserts at **Café Giolitti**.
- **Afternoon (3 PM)**: Visit **Galleria Borghese** (reservation required €10).
- **Evening (7 PM)**: Dinner at **Ristorante Aroma**, featuring views of the Colosseum.

### Day 5: Festivals and Events
- **May 1st (11 AM)**: Participate in **Primo Maggio concert** at Piazza San Giovanni.
- **Lunch (1 PM)**: Street food at **Campo de' Fiori Market**.
- **Afternoon (3 PM)**: Explore various events of **Notte dei Musei** on May 23rd.
- **Evening (7 PM)**: Dinner at **Hostaria I Ponzio** specializing in seafood.

### Day 6: Shopping and Leisure
- **Morning (10 AM)**: Visit the **Spanish Steps** and shop at boutiques.
- **Lunch (1 PM)**: Light meal at **Caffè Propaganda**.
- **Afternoon (3 PM)**: Explore **Via del Corso** for Italian fashion.
- **Evening (8 PM)**: Dinner at **Ristorante Metamorfosi**.

## Events in May
- **Festa della Repubblica (June 2)**: Preparations start in late May.
- **Taste of Roma (Mid-May)**: Annual gourmet food festival.
- **Italian Open (May 6 - May 15)**: Major tennis event with daily matches.
